# EPA TRI Community Health and Demographics Tracker

## What It Is

A cross-linked database connecting EPA Toxic Release Inventory facility data to county-level health outcomes and demographics across all 50 US states. For any TRI-reporting facility, see not just what chemicals are released and in what quantities, but the health outcomes and demographics of the surrounding community. No free, public database currently provides this cross-linkage at national scale.

## Key Statistics

- **22,936 facilities** reporting toxic chemical releases across all 50 US states
- **155,814 chemical release records** covering 571 distinct chemicals (2022-2023 reporting years)
- **6.5 billion pounds** of total toxic releases tracked
- **44,019 known carcinogen release records** totaling 657.5 million pounds
- **3,143 US counties** linked with health outcomes and demographic data
- **4,480 parent companies** across 30 industry sectors
- **0.847 average quality score** (out of 1.0) across all facility records

## Why It Matters

Environmental justice research has documented for decades that polluting facilities are disproportionately sited in low-income communities and communities of color. But the data needed to study these disparities at national scale is scattered across multiple disconnected government databases:

- EPA TRI Explorer shows facility releases — but not community health context.
- County Health Rankings shows health outcomes — but not pollution sources.
- Census data shows demographics — but not environmental context.

This database bridges those gaps. For the first time, researchers, advocates, journalists, and policymakers can query a single database to answer questions like: What chemicals are released in my county, and what are the health outcomes here? Which companies release the most carcinogens, and in what kinds of communities?

## Sample Insights

- **Nevada leads the nation in carcinogen releases** at 261.7 million pounds, driven largely by mining operations — more than four times the carcinogen release volume of the next-highest state, Texas (58.9 million lbs).
- **Texas has the most TRI-reporting facilities** of any state (2,084), followed by Ohio (1,386), California (1,210), Pennsylvania (1,106), and Illinois (1,026).
- **Lead and lead compounds remain among the highest-volume toxic releases** at 832 million pounds nationally, despite decades of regulation — raising questions about enforcement effectiveness.
- **The Chemicals sector has the most TRI-reporting facilities** (3,579), followed by Fabricated Metals (3,016) and Nonmetallic Mineral Products (2,843), showing the breadth of industries with significant toxic release profiles.

## Data Sources

All data comes from public government sources:

| Source | What It Provides |
|--------|-----------------|
| EPA Toxic Release Inventory | Facility locations, chemical releases, parent companies, industry codes |
| County Health Rankings | Premature death rates, poor health %, low birthweight, smoking, obesity, life expectancy |
| Census American Community Survey | Population, income, poverty rate, demographics, education |

## How to Access the Data

- **Interactive Dashboard** — Search and filter by state, county, chemical, company, or industry. Visualize trends and compare communities.
- **Bulk Exports** — Download the full dataset or filtered subsets in CSV, JSON, or Excel format.
- **SQLite Database** — Direct access to the underlying database for advanced analysis.
- **Methodology Documentation** — Full documentation of data sources, cross-linkage methods, quality scoring, and known limitations.
