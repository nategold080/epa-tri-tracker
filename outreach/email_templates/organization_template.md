**What the tool does:**

For any county or state, you can look up:
- Which industrial facilities report toxic chemical releases under the EPA TRI program
- What chemicals they release, in what quantities, and whether those chemicals are known carcinogens
- The health outcomes in that county (premature death rates, rates of poor health, low birthweight, and more)
- The demographic and economic profile of that county (poverty rates, income levels, population)

This information currently exists across multiple disconnected government databases. Our tool brings it together in one place so that advocates, organizers, and communities can see the full picture without needing to be data scientists.

**By the numbers:**
- 22,936 TRI-reporting facilities across all 50 states
- 155,814 chemical release records (571 distinct chemicals)
- 44,019 known carcinogen release records totaling 657.5 million pounds
- Health outcomes linked for 3,143 US counties
- 30 industry sectors and 4,480 parent companies tracked

**How your organization could use this:**
- Identify the worst-polluting facilities in the communities you serve
- Connect specific chemical releases to local health outcomes for advocacy materials
- Support testimony, public comments, or litigation with facility-level data
- Search by state, county, chemical name, industry, or parent company
- Download data in spreadsheet-friendly formats (CSV, Excel) for your own analysis

The tool is completely free. Data exports, the interactive dashboard, and the underlying database are all available at no cost. The data comes entirely from public government sources (EPA, Census Bureau, County Health Rankings).

I built this project because I believe environmental health data should be accessible to the communities most affected by pollution, not locked behind technical barriers.
