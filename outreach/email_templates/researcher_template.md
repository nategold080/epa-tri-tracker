**What the dataset is:**

The EPA TRI Community Health and Demographics Tracker is a cross-linked database of 22,936 EPA Toxic Release Inventory facilities across all 50 US states, with 155,814 chemical release records (2022-2023), joined to county-level health outcomes from County Health Rankings and demographic data from the Census American Community Survey. For any TRI-reporting facility, you can see not just what chemicals are released and in what quantities, but the health outcomes, poverty rates, and demographic composition of the surrounding county.

No freely available, persistent database currently links facility-level toxic releases to community health and demographic context at this national scale. EPA's TRI Explorer shows facility releases but not community health context. County Health Rankings shows health outcomes but not pollution sources. Our database bridges this gap.

**Key stats:**
- 22,936 facilities across all 50 states
- 155,814 chemical release records covering 571 distinct chemicals
- 44,019 records flagged as known carcinogen releases (657.5 million lbs total)
- 6.5 billion total pounds of toxic releases tracked
- Cross-linked to health outcomes for 3,143 US counties
- 4,480 unique parent companies across 30 industry sectors
- 0.847 average quality score (out of 1.0)
